- They'd all heard the voice, but only [[Lonescar]] had seen the [[Great Hook]]
- [[Hafsah]] spotted a [[Kittensnake]] hiding in a burrow
- So did the Great Claw which descended upon the thing
- The monster lived up to its name

Scene change

- [[Tall Tale]] retelling the story, and for the first time the fey court of [[Nod]] was silent
- "They don't get eaten by the Great Claw at this time. At this time, anyway."
- A dignified lady of the court asked, "What?"
- "The Great Hook doesn't get 'em. I'm explaining this to you all cause you looked nervous."
- He gave a side-eye
- "Maybe some of us were a little concerned, but it's not the same thing."
- "Well if you want me to stop-"
- "No, no, no, if you may continue to chant..."
- Tall Tale then found where he was in the story

Back to the past

- There was a splatter of red
- It was the party, six including companions, eight including [[Abzi]] and [[Telemara|her lion]], though they were injured
- [[Bravemeek]] took the opportunity to teleport past the monster in the direction of the [[Venom Moon Clan]] camp, taking Abzi's lion instead of her at her insistence
- The others followed suit and began to flee, Hafsah shooting an arrow which seemed almost to provoke the thing
- [[Reokosh]] leapt toward the thing to rodeo it, and fortunately the arrow managed to tease the Great Hook into an advantageous position
- Lonescar saw this and was stunned into silence. Eventually he regained his composure and threw a smoke bomb at it
- While Reokosh rode the thing, she felt a chill creep up her spine, she suppressed the feeling
- Bravemeek kept running. As did Hafsah and [[Kefirah]] (the former after a moment's hesitation)
- Bravemeek recalled [[Glow]] to him and recounted his disappointment, that it was just getting good, that a thing was gonna get the dinosaur from the sky
- Hafsah saw it, Lonescar saw it: a wyvern
- In addition, in the distance they heard horns and saw banners unfurl. [[Ape Clan]], as Hafsah later confirmed. Quick, crafty, and with tools. Likely here to attack and take, get in and get out fast
- The wyvern grabbed ahold of the dinosaur with Reokosh still on it and she took a firmer grip on the Great Hook's head to avoid falling off
- Lonescar evaluated the situation, that the wyvern hadn't noticed the dragonborn as it stung and killed the Great Hook and flew up to a lethal height. He had never seen someone so stupid in his entire life, that from the moment Reokosh charged the Great Hook she should've been bit clean in half. But he saw her wrestle it, successfully, not die, and despite everything as the situation devolved so incredibly and so rapidly, he saw her persist. 
	- He wanted to utilize this stupidity later

Tall Tale and the Court

- The court was panicking
- "I gotta step out for a quick lunch, mind if we take a break?"
- After a brief recess

Past

- Lonescar tossed a ritual shell, magic running in reverse for those who he didn't know he saw as peers. So the silence spell amplified noise instead
- Hafsah looked behind her to see just about everything in this section of Planegea was following her
- Reokosh, having noticed the wyvern noticed her, leapt to the canopy, avoiding all retributive strikes from both the wyvern and the still-just-barely-alive Great Hook
- Bravemeek cast a spell causing the ape clan and the wyvern to veer off as they approached a gathering of Dragonborn canoeing about an [[The Unfalls|Unfall]]
- However, the wyvern had dropped the Great Hook, which continued its restless pursuit
- Hafsah slipped into a shallow tar pit as she fired back at the dinosaur, Lonescar tied a rope between her and Reokosh (who paused to fire her breath weapon back) as they ran to free her
- With a final charge led by Bravemeek, the party got away
- It was getting dark out, however, and Bravemeek suggested they sleep out in a nearby shelter. Though it would be near the site of some [[Spellskin]] sightings
- Reokosh suggested going to kill the Great Hook, that none would dare to mess with them if they did (wrong). Lonescar took the opportunity to bicker some with her
- However, at this point in time, Hafsah noticed a small mark on Reokosh. Venom from the wyvern's stinger, which took effect (4 levels of exhaustion). She insisted on searching for tracks, though she was in a state of sort of inebriation and the others decided to take the opportunity to lead her to shelter, as per Bravemeek's suggestion

![[Spellskin Cave.png]]

- Before they left, they were told of the wizard [[Dekuki]] who had some great beast with two terrible eyes, but they saw now the day star shone through one hole in the ceiling, the moon through the other; merely a tall tale
- It was quiet all except for a shuffling of steps, invisible servants. Lonescar also noticed some wood shaped into an arrangement resembling a human skull
- Much of their work (re: scaring away humanoids) was already done. Lonescar set up some additional alerts toward the front, leaving the ceiling mostly unguarded, but it was unlikely anyone'd be able to get up there anyways

- Bravemeek spoke with [[Glow]] during first watch and wondered about how long [[Tall Tale]] wanted them to stick around Planegea
- Glow pointed out that earlier he'd seen one of the hills move, but neither thought it was cause for concern, as surely they just did that from time to time, as in [[Nod]]

- Hafsah's watch passed by uneventfully

- Lonescar awoke to a strange noise and almost instinctively snuffed out the fire
- In the distance he saw a humanoid walking through the jungle, not much larger than Lonescar himself, writhing back and forth on the edge of visibility before veering toward water, toward [[Lion Clan]]
- As he quietly approached, holding the figure at the edge of his sight, he noticed he walked through footprints eerily similar to his own
- When he got back to camp, he found Glow was awake and asked them if they'd seen the thing
- He didn't have much useful to say, having not seen anything

- In the night, a living *fabricate* spell destroyed Reokosh's [[Untamed Cloak]] and in its place she received a [[Roar-Breaker]] (unbeknownst to her until later) in the form of an arm band

In the morning

- Lonescar informed everyone of what he'd seen and told them not to follow his tracks if they didn't see him
